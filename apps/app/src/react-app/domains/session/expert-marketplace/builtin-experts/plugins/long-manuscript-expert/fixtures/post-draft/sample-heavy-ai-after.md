AI is starting to change HR work at the points where teams lose the most time:
screening repeated resumes, turning interview notes into comparable evidence,
and spotting training gaps before they become performance problems. The value
is not that the tool sounds modern. The value is that it removes a specific
piece of friction from recruiting, training, or organization management.

For enterprise buyers, the next question is operational. Which HR step should
be automated first, what data can safely support it, and who checks the result
before it affects a person? A useful AI+HR plan should answer those questions
before it promises transformation.
